01 — Context
What Errands is

Errands solves a problem every Nigerian knows — the inability to trust a stranger with your tasks, your money, or your time. Whether it's sending someone to the market, picking up a package, doing a school run, or holding funds for a transaction you're not sure about — trust has always been the missing piece.

Errands is two products in one: a vetted errand marketplace where verified runners handle your tasks, and Errands Escrow, a secure payment holding service for any transaction where trust is the barrier.

The product is built to global standard from day one. Nigeria is the proof of concept. The same trust problem exists in Dubai, Singapore, London, and Lagos — and the plan is to follow it.

02 — What to test
Your testing flow
Test both as a user and a runner. Use the live link at the top of this page. Use your real email — accounts are saved to a real database.
If something looks broken — try again tomorrow
The product is being updated in real time. Bugs you find today may already be fixed. Note what you find and when — not wait for a perfect product before giving feedback.
1
Open the landing page
Read the landing page from top to bottom. Does it communicate what Errands is within 10 seconds? Does it feel credible and global or local and basic?
2
Sign up as a user
Create an account with your email — or tap Continue with Google to sign up instantly with your Google account. Select your city and neighbourhood from the dropdown. If using email, confirm via the link sent to your inbox.
3
Explore the home dashboard
Look at the dashboard layout. Tap the KO avatar — it opens your profile menu. Check wallet balance shows. Is the layout clean and purposeful?
4
Post a test errand
Tap Post an errand. Select an errand type from the dropdown — notice how the location fields change based on what you select. Fill in all 3 steps. Step 3 shows a rate guide and summary before you post.
5
Explore Wallet and Escrow
Tap Wallet in the bottom nav — fund a test amount, check the transaction card. Then tap Escrow — review the three tiers, create a test escrow transaction. Note: no real money moves in this prototype.
6
Access the runner experience
One account works for both user and runner. To sign up as a runner — from the landing page tap "Earn as a runner." Use a second email. After confirming your email you land on the runner dashboard automatically.
7
Explore the runner dashboard and Academy
Once signed in as a runner, explore the dashboard. Tap Academy to access training — complete at least 2 of the 5 modules. Review the growth pathway: Rookie → Certified → Specialist → Premium → Neighbourhood Captain.
8
Find Help, Referral and Legal pages
Tap Profile in the bottom nav — the profile menu has links to Help & Support, Refer & Earn, Terms and Privacy. Tap each and review. Help & Support has 5 issue categories.
9
Access the Admin panel
On the sign in screen, tap the Errands logo 3 times quickly. The admin panel opens. You will see live stats — total users, runners, errands. Pending runner verifications appear with Approve and Reject buttons.
10
Check the legal pages
From the profile menu, open Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Pricing, and Dispute Resolution. Are these substantial enough for a financial platform?
11
Come back and fill this form
Your feedback below goes directly to Kenneth's dashboard. Be honest. The more specific you are, the more useful it is.
